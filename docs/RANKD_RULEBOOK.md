# RANKD Rulebook

Initial Launch Version: 0.1  
Game: EA NHL 26, EASHL 6v6  
Format: RANKD Elo Matchmaking

## 1. Purpose

RANKD is a competitive 6v6 EASHL matchmaking server built around fair games, clear structure, and Elo-based progression. The goal is to give players a reliable place to queue, compete, track results, and improve their ranking through actual performance.

All players are expected to compete in good faith, respect other players, and follow the match setup process created by the RANKD bot and staff.

## 2. Player Eligibility

Players must be members of the RANKD Discord server to participate.

Players must use their own account and may not queue, play, or report results for another player.

Players must follow Discord server rules, EA NHL conduct rules, and all RANKD staff instructions during league activity.

Staff may remove or restrict any player who is disruptive, abusive, dishonest, or harmful to the competitive environment.

## 3. Match Format

RANKD launch matches are played in EA NHL 26 EASHL 6v6 club format.

Each match requires 12 total players:

- 2 Centers
- 2 Left Wings
- 2 Right Wings
- 2 Left Defense
- 2 Right Defense
- 2 Goalies

Each team consists of:

- Center
- Left Wing
- Right Wing
- Left Defense
- Right Defense
- Goalie

The bot will only create a match once every position has enough players.

## 4. Queue Rules

Players join the queue using the RANKD bot.

Players must queue for the position they intend to play.

The queue is first come, first served by position. If more than two players are queued at the same position, the first two players at that position are guaranteed for the next match. Extra players remain in queue for a future match.

Players may not queue multiple times or attempt to bypass queue order.

Players who are already in an active match may not queue for another match until their active match is closed or completed.

Players should leave the queue if they are no longer available to play.

## 5. Match Room Process

Once a valid 12-player match is created, the bot will assign the match to an available RANKD match room.

If all reusable match rooms are full, the bot may create a temporary overflow text channel for the match.

Only players assigned to the match and authorized staff may access the match room.

Players must use the match room to coordinate setup, teams, server selection, and match readiness.

Match rooms are temporary competitive spaces. Players should keep discussion focused on the match.

## 6. Server Selection

RANKD uses the following server options:

- East
- Central
- West

After a match is created, players will vote on the server in the private match room.

The voting window is 2 minutes.

If all assigned players vote before the timer ends, the vote may close early.

If not all players vote within 2 minutes, the server will be selected based on the votes that were submitted.

If there is a tie, East is the default server.

If no players vote, East is the default server.

## 7. Player Responsibilities

Players are expected to:

- Be ready when they join the queue.
- Play the position they queued for.
- Join the match setup promptly once selected.
- Communicate respectfully.
- Follow the selected server result.
- Complete the game unless there is a valid issue.
- Report problems to staff instead of escalating arguments.

Players may not intentionally delay, troll, throw, quit without reason, or disrupt match setup.

## 8. Staff Assistance

If a match room has an issue that players cannot resolve, players may use the Call Staff feature when available.

Staff alerts should be used for real issues, including:

- Player no-show
- Player refusing assigned position
- Server dispute
- Harassment or toxic behavior
- Match setup breakdown
- Possible cheating or rule abuse

Abusing staff alerts may result in penalties.

## 9. Match Completion And Reporting

Matches must be completed unless staff determines the match should be canceled or replayed.

Final results must be reported accurately.

If automated stat collection is active, official stats may be pulled from the approved stat source.

If automated stat collection is not available, staff may require screenshots, video, or confirmation from players.

False reporting, altered screenshots, or dishonest result claims may result in severe penalties.

## 10. Elo And Records

All players begin with 2500 Elo unless staff announces otherwise.

Elo changes are based on match results and the active RANKD rating system.

Wins, losses, and Elo updates become official once the match result is accepted by the bot or staff.

Staff may correct rating issues caused by bot errors, incorrect reporting, or confirmed disputes.

Players may not demand manual Elo adjustments unless there is a clear error.

## 11. Disconnections And No-Shows

Players are responsible for having a stable connection before joining queue.

If a player disconnects before the match begins, staff may decide whether to wait, replace, cancel, or close the match.

If a player disconnects during the match, players should continue unless both teams and staff agree otherwise.

Repeated disconnects, no-shows, or queue abandonment may lead to temporary queue restrictions.

## 12. Substitutions

Players may not replace themselves without staff approval once a match is created.

Any substitution must be approved by staff to protect Elo integrity and queue fairness.

A substitute must play the position being replaced unless staff approves an exception.

## 13. Conduct

RANKD is competitive, but players must remain respectful.

The following behavior is not allowed:

- Hate speech or slurs
- Harassment or threats
- Excessive personal attacks
- Intentionally throwing games
- Collusion or win trading
- Impersonating another player
- Cheating or exploiting
- Repeated match disruption
- Staff harassment

Trash talk may happen in competitive environments, but staff will act when it becomes abusive, discriminatory, or disruptive.

## 14. Competitive Integrity

Players may not manipulate the queue, Elo system, match results, or staff process.

Examples of competitive integrity violations include:

- Queue dodging to avoid certain players
- Coordinating false reports
- Playing on another player account
- Intentionally losing to help another player
- Using unauthorized gameplay exploits
- Creating unnecessary disputes to avoid a loss

Confirmed violations may result in rating resets, match forfeits, suspensions, or bans.

## 15. Staff Authority

RANKD staff may interpret and enforce these rules as needed.

Staff may make judgment calls for situations not yet covered by this rulebook.

Staff decisions should prioritize fairness, competitive integrity, and the health of the server.

Players may appeal staff decisions respectfully, but arguing in public channels may lead to additional discipline.

## 16. Penalties

Possible penalties include:

- Warning
- Queue restriction
- Match forfeit
- Temporary suspension
- Elo correction
- Season removal
- Server ban

Penalty severity depends on the situation, player history, intent, and impact on the match or community.

## 17. Rulebook Updates

This rulebook may be updated as RANKD develops.

Rule changes should be announced clearly in Discord.

Staff may apply emergency rulings before the rulebook is updated if a new issue affects competitive integrity.

## 18. Launch Notes

This is the initial launch rulebook for RANKD.

The rulebook should be reviewed after early testing and adjusted based on real match flow, bot behavior, staff workload, and player feedback.
